Source: docs/demo-script-F-polished.md (locked 2026-04-25 evening pass).
Convention: one sentence per line. Paragraph break = beat boundary. All numbers spelled out. Em-dashes preserved as TTS pause markers. Parenthetical "(pause)" markers indicate ~0.8 second silences to splice in post-generation if the model compresses them. Do not feed the parentheticals to the TTS model — strip them or replace with extra punctuation.

----- BEAT 1 — Cold open and problem statement (target 0:00–0:20, ~42 words, ~17 s) -----

In my day job, I help weather satellites see better.
From thirty-six thousand kilometers up, they hover above roughly the same place — and they monitor Europe's weather, all at once.
But as far as they can see, they could not be further from seeing the weather in the eyes of us humans.
Because the weather that matters to you, is the weather in your view.

----- BEAT 2 — The transition (target 0:20–0:55, ~64 words, ~25 s) -----

Humanity carries around billions of mobile webcams.
We share images with friends and family every day — but what if we could also share the conditions in our view?
How is the swell today?
What are the alpine conditions?
Is there still snow on the lone summit?
Weather apps are great — they make sense of millions of parameters.
VYU uses those, and lets you add your own view, with visual reasoning for everyone.

----- BEAT 3 — Wiring middle, the photo (target 0:55–1:15, ~51 words, ~20 s) -----

Behind every cone, three Claude Managed Agents do the work — but on two clocks.
Two run when you submit.
The first reads your photo.
It opens a managed session with a skill that knows what weather looks like — the phenomenon, the features, the visible signal — written down before you put your phone away.

----- BEAT 4 — Wiring middle, reconciliation and memory (target 1:15–1:40, ~59 words, ~23 s) -----

The second managed agent runs at the same time — Reconciliation.
It reads your photo against the radar, the satellite, and the official forecast — all pulled in by the edge function and handed to the agent in one session.
What agrees, what disagrees, where the uncertainty sits.
Every verified report drops into a memory box keyed to the place.
The map remembers what other observers nearby saw.

----- BEAT 5 — Wiring middle, the orchestrator (target 1:40–2:00, ~59 words, ~23 s) -----

The third managed agent runs when you ask.
Deep Researcher reads everything — your verified report, the memory box for this place, and the memory box for you.
The fact in the answer — that another contributor here noted the cool wind off the lake — is in the answer because that contributor put it there.
The agent does not re-derive it.
It inherits.

----- BEAT 6 — The trade (target 2:00–2:25, ~80 words, ~32 s) -----

You took a photo of your sky and asked where to find a beer.
The map named the dark Doppelbock the place is known for, and warned about the long sleeve once the sun drops behind the Heiliger Berg.
You asked about a Turkish restaurant on holiday.
The map told you about a waterfall eight kilometers away you had not heard of.
(pause)
One photo.
One question.
Tit for tat.

----- BEAT 7 — Validation (target 2:25–2:45, ~33 words, ~13 s) -----

This is the supervised benchmark.
The Classifier hits these numbers on a held-out set, with thresholds set before the runs.
We do not claim it is perfect.
We claim it is honest.

----- BEAT 8 — Close (target 2:45–3:00, ~44 words, ~17 s) -----

The satellites see the continent.
The people see the moment.
The map composes them.
(pause)
It is not much, but it is something — and even the smallest gesture, when shared, has true value.
VYU. What's in your view.
